### Fixes

- **Dual-motor covers: stopping the cover now actually stops a running tilt motor, and the dedicated tilt-stop button works**: on a cover with a **separate tilt motor**, a plain tilt move (`set_tilt_position`, tilt open/close) tracked only that a tilt motor was running internally — `async_stop_cover` never consulted that state, so a user stop froze the position tracker but left the tilt relay energized and the motor running unattended. Separately, the `STOP_TILT` service/button was advertised but never implemented, so pressing it was a silent no-op. `async_stop_cover` now also settles a plain dual-motor tilt move (unless the tilt relay itself is the one reporting, where a stop must not be echoed back at it), and `async_stop_cover_tilt` is implemented by delegating to the same stop — including on shared-motor tilt strategies, where the tilt phase is the travel motor and the existing stop already covers it. Because a plain tilt move leaves the last *travel* command set while the travel motor stays idle, settling the tilt is careful not to also pulse that idle travel relay — which on **Toggle** hardware is itself a movement command ([#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)) and on a **Pulse** cover set not to send an endpoint stop a go-to-favourite reposition ([#133](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/133)); **Switch** mode, whose latched travel relay must always be de-energized, still stops it.
- **A tilt-stop pressed from outside while the cover is moving no longer runs the cover to its end**: on a cover with a **separate tilt motor**, pressing a tilt-stop from outside Home Assistant (a wall button, or the physical tilt-stop input) while Home Assistant was itself running a travel move used to be swallowed. The integration deliberately suppresses relay stops for anything triggered externally — but here nothing external had stopped the *travel* motor; that move was Home Assistant's own, and only the tilt relay had reported. The position tracker froze while the latched travel relay kept driving the cover to its physical limit. Home Assistant now recognises that when only the tilt relay reports and a self-initiated travel move is still running, its own travel motor still needs stopping, and stops it — the mirror image of the tilt-motor case above.
- **Changing tilt direction now stops the right motor and settles before reversing**: reversing a tilt mid-move — dragging the tilt slider back the other way, or pressing tilt open while it is still closing — had three faults on covers with a **separate tilt motor** or **toggle** wiring. The stop it sent was aimed at the *travel* motor, keyed off the last travel command, so on **Toggle (opposite button)** covers it pulsed an *idle travel relay* — which on that hardware is itself a movement command, starting a phantom travel (a [#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)-class fault). An endpoint tilt reversal (tilt open while it was closing, or the reverse) sent the new direction with no stop and no pause at all, and a tilt command that happened to land on the position the slats were animating through was silently dropped instead of stopping them. Tilt reversals now stop the tilt axis itself — never the idle travel relay — then wait out the same fixed 1.0 s settle gap that travel reversals use so the motor comes to rest before being driven the other way, and a press on the current animated tilt position is treated as the stop it is. Tilt driven from outside Home Assistant (a wall switch reversing the slats) is unaffected: the hardware handles its own stop-and-reverse and the integration only tracks it, so no relay is pulsed and no phantom echo is marked.
- **Toggle covers: an abandoned tilt pre-step no longer pulses an idle tilt motor**: on a **toggle**-wired cover with a **separate tilt motor**, a tilt move that finished at a tilt endpoint self-stops on the hardware's own limit — the integration correctly skips sending a relay stop there, but it also forgot to clear which direction the tilt had last moved. If a later command started a *travel* pre-step (tilt requested past **max tilt allowed position**, so travel runs first with the tilt motor sitting idle) and something then abandoned that pre-step — a fresh position or tilt command arriving before travel finished — the abandon path unconditionally sent a tilt stop, and toggle's stop is a relay pulse keyed off that stale leftover direction: it started the idle tilt motor rather than stopping anything (a [#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)-class phantom pulse). The stale direction is now cleared as soon as a tilt-motor move finishes, and the abandon path only sends a tilt stop when the tilt motor was actually driven during the phase being abandoned.
- **Separate tilt motor: an external tilt-switch press no longer crashes before the tilt is calibrated**: a **separate tilt motor** cover can be set up with its tilt switches wired before its tilt open/close times are entered — a normal, supported step in getting a cover configured. In that window a physical press on a tilt switch crashed the integration with an `AttributeError` in every control mode (**Switch**, **Pulse**, both **Toggle** modes), because the external-tilt handlers all assume the tilt calibration they need already exists. Tilt-switch events are now ignored — logged, not acted on — until tilt times are set, exactly like every other control path already treats an unconfigured tilt axis.
- **Setting a position no longer re-pulses a motor already driving a shared-motor tilt move**: on a cover whose tilt is simulated on the travel motor itself (**Inline** or **Sequential** tilt mode), calling `set_position` while a tilt-open/close move was already running that motor only checked whether *travel* was tracked as moving — it wasn't, tilt was — so a same-direction call re-issued the start command. On **Toggle** hardware a second press of the same direction button while the motor is already running is read as *stop*, not *go*: the motor actually halted there, while the tracker carried on believing it was travelling, and a later automatic stop pulse then restarted it unsupervised, running the cover on to an endpoint nobody asked for. `set_position` now recognises a running shared-motor tilt move as "the motor is running" for both its direction-change stop and its same-direction retarget decisions, so it stops-and-settles on a reversal and retargets in place on a same-direction call, exactly as it already did for travel. Covers with a **separate tilt motor** are unaffected — that motor is independent of travel and was never in scope.
- **Dual-motor covers: finishing or abandoning a tilt phase no longer pulses an idle travel relay at an endpoint**: on a cover with a **separate tilt motor**, moving the cover drives the slats to a safe position, runs the travel motor, then restores the slats — and a stray *travel* stop was fired at two boundaries in that sequence even when the travel motor was already idle or self-stopped at its physical limit. On **Toggle (opposite button)** (momentary) hardware a travel "stop" is a *pulse* of the opposite travel relay, so sending it to a stopped motor is itself a movement command ([#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)); on a **Pulse** cover set not to send an endpoint stop it is read as a go-to-favourite reposition ([#133](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/133)). Reproduced on a dual-motor toggle-opposite close: the motor self-stopped at 0%, the tilt-restore step's stop then pulsed the open relay, and the cover physically re-opened while the integration still reported it closed. The same stray stop also fired when a tilt-to-safe pre-step — whose travel motor never started — was abandoned by a fresh command arriving before travel began. Both lifecycle stops now pass through the same endpoint / self-stop gating the ordinary auto-stop already uses: the travel stop is skipped when the travel motor never ran during the phase being ended, or when it is settled at an endpoint it self-stops at, while a genuine mid-travel stop is still sent — and **Switch** mode, whose latched relay must always be de-energized at the endpoints, is unchanged.
- **Dual-motor covers: an externally-triggered multi-phase move no longer fires relays at the phase boundary**: on a cover with a **separate tilt motor**, some moves happen in two phases — a tilt-to-safe step before the cover travels, or (when the slats are only allowed below a certain position) a travel step before the tilt. When *you* start such a move from Home Assistant the integration drives each phase itself; when a **wall button or remote** starts it, the hardware drives both phases and Home Assistant only tracks the motion. That track-only rule held for the first phase but broke at the boundary into the second: the continuation runs on the integration's own background updater — a context that had lost the "this was externally triggered" mark — so it fired real relay commands (stopping the tilt motor and pulsing the travel relay, or vice versa) at hardware that was already moving on its own. On latching relays that means energizing a motor against its mechanical limit or fighting the wall switch. The second phase is now recognised as externally triggered across the boundary, so an external multi-phase move stays purely track-only from start to finish while still following the hardware into its second phase (rather than stalling and needing a second press). Moves you start from Home Assistant are unaffected — they drive every phase exactly as before.
- **A calibration that times out no longer permanently drops the startup-delay compensation**: calibrating a travel or tilt **startup delay** temporarily zeroes that compensation for the duration of the test, so the position tracker doesn't cancel out the very overhead being measured, and restores it once the test ends. That restore only ran when the test was stopped or cancelled normally — a calibration that instead ran past its timeout left the startup delay zeroed for good, so every move afterwards silently drifted the tracked position with no indication anything was wrong. The startup delay is now always put back, whether the calibration finishes, is cancelled, times out, or the entity is removed while a calibration is still in progress.
- **Finishing a travel/tilt-time calibration on momentary hardware no longer pulses the relay after the motor has already stopped**: the travel/tilt-time calibration protocol is "press Finish once the cover reaches the endpoint", so on **Toggle**, **Toggle (opposite button)** and **Pulse** hardware the motor has already self-stopped at its limit an instant before you press Finish (or before a calibration times out). Sending a stop pulse there is itself a movement command on that hardware — on **Toggle (opposite button)** it pulsed the opposite relay and drove the cover back off the endpoint, on same-button **Toggle** it restarted the motor, and on **Pulse** with **Send endpoint stop** disabled it repositioned to the configured favourite. Finish and timeout now skip that redundant stop on hardware that self-stops at its endpoints; **Switch** mode's latched relay is unaffected and still de-energizes as before, and cancelling a calibration still always stops the motor.

## 4.9.0 (2026-07-20)

### Features

- **Wrapped covers: new "Invert position" option for covers that run backwards** ([#160](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/160)): some covers are wired or built the opposite way round from a standard shutter — an overhanging awning, say, whose underlying entity reports *open* when the awning is fully extended (shading the window) and *closed* when it is rolled away. Wrapping such an entity gave a time-based cover whose whole position axis read backwards: the UI's open button retracted the awning, 100% meant stowed, and the tracker followed the underlying entity's inverted sense. A new per-cover wrapped-cover option, **Invert position** (default **off**, the unchanged behaviour), flips that axis end to end: the reported position becomes `100 −` the wrapped entity's position, the open / close commands are swapped, `set_cover_position(p)` is forwarded as `100 − p`, and the underlying entity's `opening` / `closing` (and command-echo) states are read in the inverted sense too — so *open* means retracted and *closed* means extended, the right way round. It flips the **position** axis; on a wrapped cover using a *timed* tilt mode (**Inline** / **Sequential**) the slats are simulated by pulsing the same — now inverted — motor, so invert is intended for position-only covers such as awnings and shutters rather than tilting venetians. Configurable from the config card (EN/PT/PL).
- **New per-cover setting — "Always re-send open/close at the endpoints"** ([#167](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/167)): the integration normally treats an `open_cover` / `close_cover` issued while the tracker already sits settled at that endpoint as redundant — it skips the move, or fires only a short resync pulse — because the motor is believed to already be there. For a cover with **no position feedback** that can *also* be driven by an external remote or wall button, that belief is untrustworthy: the remote moved the cover without telling Home Assistant, so HA still shows "closed" while the shutter is physically open, and the close command it decides to skip is exactly the one needed to fix it. The cover then appears unresponsive — it only starts obeying again after you nudge it off the endpoint by hand. A new per-cover option, **Always re-send open/close at the endpoints** (default **off**, the unchanged behaviour), makes an endpoint command always reach the motor: the move is modelled as starting from the *opposite* endpoint, so it runs for the full travel time (including each mode's tilt phases) rather than being skipped or shortened. Available in every control mode — **Switch**, **Pulse**, both **Toggle** modes and **wrapped** covers — and configurable from the config card (EN/PT/PL). Leave it **off** for covers that report their own position, where the skip is the correct behaviour and a forced re-drive would just run the motor into its limit on every redundant press.
- **The configuration card remembers the device you were working on** ([#169](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/169)): the card's device picker reset itself on every load, so reloading the dashboard — or coming back to it later — meant re-picking the cover you were in the middle of configuring or calibrating. The selection is now remembered and restored automatically. It is stored per browser (not per Home Assistant user), and validated against the live entity list on restore, so a cover that has since been deleted is dropped rather than restored into a picker with a failing config load beneath it. A picker you deliberately cleared stays cleared for that card, and a device you pick yourself while the restore is still resolving is never overridden. Browsers that block or disable local storage simply fall back to the previous behaviour of starting empty.

### Fixes

- **Reversing a moving cover: the settle gap stays fixed at 1.0 second** ([#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)): reversing is never a single step — the motor is stopped, given a moment to come to rest, and only then driven the other way. That pause was briefly made configurable per cover during the 4.9.0 release candidates, on the theory that 1.0 second was too short for some motors. The hardware it was added for turned out to reverse correctly at 1.0 second — the value every earlier release already used — so the change fixed nothing, and the only thing the setting demonstrably enabled was choosing a *shorter* gap, which breaks covers: the reverse command arrives before the motor has stopped (or, on momentary-relay hardware, before the relay has released), so it is ignored while the position tracker runs on and the entity desyncs from the cover. In **Toggle (opposite button)** mode that desync is worse than a parked cover, because a "stop" there is a pulse of the opposite relay: it stops a moving motor, but on an already-stopped one it is a movement command, so the next stop can drive the cover to its endpoint. The gap is therefore fixed at 1.0 second again, which also comfortably outlasts any legitimate relay pulse window. Configurations that still carry `direction_change_delay` — from a release candidate, or in YAML — keep loading, and the value is ignored. If reversals do not take on your hardware, shorten your *device's* pulse length instead (a few hundred milliseconds; motor manuals generally require well under a second) — that is the setting that governs when the relay releases.
- **Wrapped covers: a wrapped entity coming back from unavailable no longer moves the position** ([#160](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/160)): when the entity a cover wraps drops out — its integration reloads, its device reconnects, Home Assistant restarts — the first state it reports on the way back is whatever that integration starts up in, which for a cover with **no position feedback** is typically a plain `closed`. That was read as the cover reporting an endpoint, and the tracked position was snapped to it, discarding the position held before the dropout. With **Invert position** switched on it was impossible to miss: the closed endpoint inverts to 100%, so a closed awning flipped to *open* in the same second it came back, several times a day, logged as `unavailable → closed → open`. A returning entity is now only trusted when it reports an actual position; a bare `closed` from one that has just reappeared is treated as the starting value it is, and the position tracked before the dropout is kept. Covers that report a real position still resync on reconnect exactly as before, and a cover settling into `closed` at the end of a normal move is unaffected — only a transition straight out of `unavailable` or `unknown`, the two states an entity that has been away comes back from, is guarded. The same guard covers **Reports commands, not endpoints**, where the retained echo was being replayed as a fresh command and ran a phantom timed travel; that mode's `unknown`-on-reconnect still freezes the tracker as before. One deliberate consequence for covers with no position feedback: if you close such a cover *by hand while Home Assistant is down*, its position is no longer quietly resynced to closed when HA returns — the position from before the restart is kept, exactly as it is for every other dropout, and the next full open or close puts the two back in step.
- **A Repairs prompt now guides you to hard-refresh after installing the card** ([#164](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/164)): Home Assistant loads dashboard resources only when the page first loads, so immediately after installing the integration the **Cover Time Based** card is missing from already-open browser sessions until the page is reloaded — and because HA's service worker caches the app, often only a *hard* refresh brings it in. Since the card is registered by the integration itself rather than installed as a standalone HACS plugin, nothing prompted you to reload. A dismissible **Settings → Repairs** notice (translated EN/PL/PT) now appears to prompt the hard refresh. It is raised only when the card resource is newly created — a first install (or a re-create after a full uninstall) — never on a version update (the card is already present and a normal reload picks up the new code) or an ordinary restart, and it clears itself on the next restart once a fresh session would load the card anyway.
- **Wrapped covers: a partial move is no longer hijacked to full open/close by the wrapped cover's own movement report** ([#165](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/165)): a time-based wrapped cover — one using **Force time-based positioning**, or wrapping an entity with no native `set_cover_position` — reaches a partial target (say 4%) by forwarding `open_cover`/`close_cover` to the underlying cover and stopping it by time. The underlying cover then reports its own `opening`/`closing` as a side effect of that command. A 0.5 s grace window already ignores that echo when it arrives promptly, but a wrapped **template** cover whose `opening`/`closing` state is derived from a physical movement `binary_sensor` can lag the relay by well over half a second (observed ~1.5 s). Arriving after the window, the echo was read as a *fresh external* open/close press and redirected the in-flight move to the endpoint — so commanding 4 % ran the shutter fully open. The wrapped handler already guarded the equivalent case for natively-forwarded `set_cover_position` moves; that guard now also covers the timed path: while a self-initiated move is running, the underlying cover's *same-direction* movement report is recognised as our own command echoing back and ignored. An *opposite-direction* report is still honoured as a genuine external reversal — e.g. the wall switch pressed the other way mid-move — so manual wall-switch tracking is unchanged.
- **A stop pressed during a wall-switch reversal is no longer swallowed** ([#173](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/173)): reversing a moving cover from a wall switch is a three-step sequence — stop the motor, wait out the **Direction change delay** so it comes to rest, then drive the other way. A stop arriving *inside* that gap — you press stop, or hit the dedicated stop button, a moment after pressing the opposite direction — was discarded, and the cover reversed anyway once the gap expired, seconds after you told it to stop. The cause is that the integration decided whether a stop was a real command by asking whether it had arrived externally, and that answer is held for the whole external call, settle gap included — so a genuine press was indistinguishable from the device echoing its own state back, and echoes must be ignored. Callers now state which they are rather than it being inferred, so a real stop claims the movement and the pending reversal drops. Anything arriving from Home Assistant — a service call, the UI, an automation — counts as a command, as do the **dedicated stop switches** (Pulse mode's stop relay, and the tilt stop relay in Switch mode and on dual-motor covers), which only ever fire because somebody pressed them. The window is the settle gap itself, so this grows more visible the higher **Direction change delay** is set — the covers on slow motors that need a wide gap ([#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)) were the most affected.
- **Commands issued from Home Assistant during a wall-switch event are no longer mistaken for the device reporting back** ([#173](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/173)): when a cover is operated externally the integration deliberately sends no relay commands — the hardware is already driving itself, and echoing the command back at it would be wrong. That suppression was applied as a plain flag held for the duration of the external event, so anything else happening in that window inherited it: a stop pressed in the Home Assistant UI halted the position tracker but sent no relay command, leaving the motor running on to the endpoint while HA showed it stopped; a move started from the UI or an automation tracked all the way to its target while firing nothing at all, so HA animated the position bar for a cover that never moved; and a move *started* externally suppressed its own endpoint stop, which on latching hardware leaves the relay energized against the mechanical limit. The suppression is now scoped to the external handler itself rather than to whatever happens to run alongside it, so it still covers exactly the echo it exists for and nothing else.
- **Dual-motor covers: an external stop now stops the tilt motor too** ([#173](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/173)): on a cover with a **separate tilt motor**, an externally triggered stop about the *travel* relay abandoned a running tilt phase but left the tilt motor energized — the tracker halted, the pending tilt restore was forgotten, and the motor kept running with nothing watching it, which on latching hardware means the slats drive to their physical limit unattended. The relay suppression that caused it is about not echoing a command back at hardware that already acted, which is true of the relay the event was about and of no other: a dedicated tilt motor is a separate relay the integration drives itself, so nothing else was ever going to take it down. The tilt stop is now sent — except when the *tilt* relay is the one reporting, where echoing it back is precisely what the suppression exists to prevent (and on toggle hardware a tilt stop is a pulse, so re-sending it would start the motor rather than stop it).
- **A superseded tilt restore no longer drives the motor behind its replacement** ([#173](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/173)): on covers whose slats share the travel motor, restoring tilt after a travel move pauses at several points — the stop command, the direction-change settle gap, the motor start — and checks after each whether it has been cancelled. That check could only tell whether *a* restore was live, not whether it was still *this* one, so a restore cancelled while paused and then replaced by a newer one read its replacement's state as its own and carried on: it drove the motor a second time and pointed the tilt tracker at the goal it had already been told to abandon, leaving the reported tilt chasing a position nothing was moving towards. Each restore now carries an identity, so a stale one fails every check meant to stop it. Two related teardown faults are fixed alongside it: a restore cancelled while its own turn-on was still in flight left the motor energized, and a restore superseded by a *new movement* stopped that replacement's motor while its tracker animated on.

## 4.8.0 (2026-07-06)

### Features

- **New "Toggle (opposite button)" control mode** ([#153](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/153)): some momentary toggle relays have no dedicated stop button and instead stop the motor when the *opposite* direction is pressed while it's moving (a same-direction press just keeps it moving); reversing then takes two presses — opposite (stop), then press again to move. The existing **Toggle** mode assumes a same-button stop, and **Pulse** mode needs a dedicated stop relay, so hardware wired this way (e.g. an **Aqara T2** in momentary-pulse mode) had no matching option. **Toggle (opposite button)** shares Toggle's state machine and options — including **Relay reports its own OFF** — differing only in which button press is read as stop, and is configurable from the config card (EN/PT/PL). (Like the other control modes beyond the legacy `is_button` flag, it is not settable from the deprecated YAML configuration.) The docs now also make clear that **Pulse** mode's stop switch is required, not optional, and point covers with no stop relay at this new mode instead.
- **Native tilt & position forwarding for wrapped covers** ([#157](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/157)): a wrapped cover whose firmware positions its own slats — for example a Z-Wave venetian shutter such as a **Shelly Wave Shutter** — is now driven by forwarding its native tilt and position commands, with the time-based tracker kept only as a live display overlay. The earlier wrapped-tilt support ([#87](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/87)) simulates tilt by pulsing the main motor for a timed burst, which is right for covers that fake tilt that way but wrong for one that positions its slats itself: a tilt-close at a travel endpoint became a no-op, intermediate angles were unreachable, and the tracker drifted because nothing read back the angle the cover reported. For an **Inline**-tilt wrapped cover whose underlying entity advertises `set_cover_tilt_position` (and `set_cover_position`), set-/open-/close-tilt are now forwarded straight to the device — the display animates during the move and snaps to the cover's reported angle on settle — `set_cover_position` is forwarded too for device-accurate positioning, the tilt display sweeps to the direction endpoint during travel (venetian slats close going down, open going up) before syncing to the reported angle, and no relay stop is issued since the device holds itself at the target. This is auto-detected from the wrapped entity's supported features — no new configuration — and the existing opt-outs (`force_time_based_position`, `ignore_reported_position`, `reports_command_not_endpoint`) still force the timed path. Covers without native tilt support, and the **Sequential** / **Separate tilt motor** strategies, keep their existing timed behaviour unchanged.

### Fixes

- **Dual-motor covers: a travel press no longer disturbs a moving tilt motor** ([#156](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/156)): in **dual-motor** tilt mode the slats have their own motor that runs independently of travel. The open/close reversal guards (and the same-button **Toggle** external handler) decided whether an incoming travel command was a stop or a reversal from the cover-level opening/closing state, which folds in tilt motion — so pressing a travel button while the *tilt* motor happened to be moving read as "the cover is already moving that way" and stopped it, or inserted the direction-change settle delay before travel. On a wall-switch (externally triggered) press that stop is tracker-only — no relay is fired — so the tilt position tracker froze while the physical tilt motor kept running, desyncing the two, plus travel started a second late. Requires the rare combination of a separate tilt motor mid-move and a simultaneous cross-axis travel press, so it was easy to miss. These decisions now key off the **travel axis** only, so an independently moving tilt motor is left alone (a travel command's own tilt-to-safe pre-step still counts, keeping the in-motion-click and reverse behaviour there). **Inline** and **sequential** tilt — where the slats share the travel motor, so a tilt phase *is* the travel motor running — are unchanged and still settle before reversing.
- **Wrapped command-echo covers now stop cleanly at both endpoints** ([#152](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/152)): a wrapped cover with **Reports commands, not endpoints** enabled typically drives an *endstop-less* motor — one with no limit switches that simply stalls against the mechanical stop while powered (e.g. a Shelly 2PM Gen4 in cover profile driving a plain tubular motor). Wrapped mode inherited the base assumption that the motor self-stops at its limits, so at the 0%/100% endpoints it skipped the stop and let the motor run — stalling for seconds until the device's own max-time cutoff on every full open/close, and running to max-time when commanded to an endpoint it was already at. It now sends the endpoint stop for these command-echo covers, exactly as **Switch** mode already de-energizes its latched relay (set **Endpoint run-on time** to 0 to cut power the instant the endpoint is reached). Separately, `open_cover` while the cover is already parked fully open now no-ops instead of re-driving to the endpoint — mirroring `close_cover` at fully closed — so it no longer re-energizes the motor with a redundant relay pulse. Wrapped covers that report a real position, and the **Switch** / **Toggle** / **Pulse** relay modes, are unchanged — they keep resyncing the tracker at the endpoints as before.

## 4.7.2 (2026-07-01)

### Fixes

- **Inline tilt restore no longer overruns on short reversals** ([#147](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/147)): in **inline** tilt mode the slats share the main travel motor, so restoring tilt after a travel move (the integration overdrives tilt to an endpoint, travels, then reverses to the requested tilt) reverses that motor. The restore issued the opposite-direction command *instantly*, while the motor was still running from the travel phase — with no settling stop and no reversal delay. For a short restore pulse the follow-up stop then arrived while the relay was still mid-reversal and was dropped, so the motor ran on to its physical endpoint instead of stopping at the restored tilt (long restores completed correctly). The restore now stops the motor and waits out the direction-change settle delay before commanding the reverse direction — the same reversal handling a normal travel direction-change already uses — so the stop is honoured and the cover holds the restored tilt. A stop (or a new movement command) issued *while a tilt restore is starting up* — during the inline settle delay, or during the dual-motor restore's stop-travel / tilt-motor-start step — now cancels the pending restore instead of being overridden when the restore resumes and re-starts the motor.

## 4.7.1 (2026-06-30)

### Fixes

- **Inline tilt at a parked endpoint no longer rolls the cover fully open/closed** ([#142](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/142)): the companion to [#125](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/125). In **inline** tilt mode the slats are articulated by the main travel motor, so a tilt move adjusting the slats while the cover is parked at a travel endpoint (fully open or fully closed) drives the motor *off* its limit switch. #125 fixed the run-on overshoot for **Switch** mode, but the self-stopping modes (**Toggle**, **Pulse** and **wrapped** covers) took a different branch: at an endpoint they skip the stop entirely, assuming the motor is still seated against its physical limit and self-stops there. For a tilt move that assumption is wrong — the motor has just been driven off the limit — so the stop was never sent and the cover ran on to the full endpoint (e.g. tilting the slats open while fully closed rolled the blind all the way up). The stop is now sent for tilt moves at an endpoint in these modes (mirroring the run-on exclusion #125 added), so the motor stops at the requested tilt; real *travel* moves to an endpoint still skip the redundant stop as before.

## 4.7.0 (2026-06-29)

### Features

- **Wrapped covers: new "Reports commands, not endpoints" option for command-echo devices** ([#137](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/137)): some underlying covers report their state as the *last command* rather than the actual position — notably single-DP Tuya roller-shutter modules (a `control` enum of open/close/stop, with no `current_position` and no `opening`/`closing` transition). They report `open` when open was commanded, `closed` when close was commanded, and `unknown` when stopped. The integration read that `closed` as the 0% endpoint and snapped the position to 0 on the close *press*, so a manual stop mid-travel was recorded as fully closed — a cover parked at ~70% jumped to closed (the issue's symptom). A new per-cover wrapped-cover option, **Reports commands, not endpoints** (default **off**, the unchanged behaviour), treats the wrapped entity's reported state as an open/close/stop *command* and tracks position purely by time, never snapping to an endpoint: `open`/`opening` → timed open, `closed`/`closing` → timed close, `unknown` → stop (freeze at the current time-based position), and `unavailable`/other → ignored. So a close press starts a timed close and a stop press freezes the cover where it is instead of snapping to 0. It is a deliberate opt-in rather than auto-detected: whether a device reports real transient moving states cannot be read from `supported_features`, and a runtime heuristic can't tell a command-echo device from an honest binary cover that only reports `closed` at the end of travel. Configurable from the config card (EN/PT/PL). (Like the other wrapped-cover options, it is not settable from the deprecated YAML configuration.)

## 4.6.0 (2026-06-28)

### Features

- **New per-cover setting — "Send stop signal at endpoints" (Pulse mode)** ([#133](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/133)): "Pulse" mode actually serves two physically opposite momentary controllers, and the 4.5.3 fix for [#129](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/129) — which made the endpoint stop **unconditional** — is right for one and wrong for the other. A **latching** controller keeps running until it receives a dedicated stop pulse, so it *needs* the endpoint stop or it gets stuck "moving" (the #129 case). An **auto-stop** controller halts itself at its 0%/100% limit switches, and a stop pulse received *while already stopped* is read as "go to favourite/preset position" (classic Somfy "my" behaviour) — so for that hardware every limit hit silently repositioned the cover (the #133 regression). Tuning the run-on delay can't help: the motor has already self-stopped, so *any* stop pulse arrives "while stopped". A new per-cover option, **Send stop signal at endpoints** (default **on**, the unchanged 4.5.3 behaviour), can be turned **off** for auto-stop controllers so the endpoint stop pulse is not sent at all; the same flag also governs a separate **tilt motor**'s endpoint stop. Configurable from the config card (EN/PT/PL) and as the `send_endpoint_stop` YAML option. As a related cleanup, the **Endpoint run-on time** field is now also shown for pulse covers that send the endpoint stop (it was silently using the 2.0s default and was unconfigurable from the card). **Switch**, **Toggle** and **wrapped-cover** modes are unchanged; the default keeps #129 fixed out of the box.

## 4.5.3 (2026-06-27)

### Fixes

- **Pulse mode sends the endpoint stop again** ([#129](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/129)): the 4.4.0 "no endpoint stop for self-stopping motors" change was too broad — it lumped **Pulse** mode in with Toggle, but the two are not the same. Toggle's "stop" re-pulses the *direction* relay (which would restart the motor), so skipping it there is correct; Pulse mode has a **dedicated stop relay** (a stop switch is required), and its momentary controller *latches* the direction command and keeps running until it receives that stop pulse. With the stop skipped at 0%/100%, the controller was left stuck "moving" — the cover only responded after several clicks, the Stop was never delivered, and the physical wall/PLC buttons appeared blocked while the controller thought it was still running. Pulse mode now pulses its stop relay at the endpoints again (deferred by **Endpoint run-on time** if configured), exactly as it did in 4.3.0; because the stop is a separate relay, it can never restart the motor. The same applies to a **separate tilt motor** — its tilt-stop relay is pulsed at the tilt endpoints too. **Toggle** and **wrapped-cover** modes are unchanged.

## 4.5.2 (2026-06-24)

### Fixes

- **Inline tilt no longer overshoots when the cover is parked at an endpoint** ([#125](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/125)): in **inline** tilt mode the slats are articulated by the main travel motor, so a tilt move drives the same relay as travel. When the cover was parked at a travel endpoint (fully open or fully closed) — the usual case when adjusting slats — a tilt move that finished there was wrongly given the **endpoint run-on**, a *travel* concept that keeps a latched relay energized so the shutter seats against its physical limit. The result: a 50% tilt that should take 0.75s (half of a 1.5s tilt time) ran for ~2s, the run-on default, overdriving the slats and starting to move the cover off the endpoint. Run-on is now suppressed for tilt moves, so the relay de-energizes as soon as the tilt target is reached. **Switch** mode is the only mode affected (it is the only one that runs on at an endpoint); travel moves to an endpoint still run on as before so the shutter seats correctly.

## 4.5.1 (2026-06-22)

### Fixes

- **Toggle mode with “Relay reports its own OFF” off: restart no longer phantom-opens the cover** ([#105](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/105)): a relay configured as not self-reporting its OFF (an **Aqara T2** in hardware-pulse mode, the case the 4.5.0 option addresses) pulses and physically releases but never tells Home Assistant, so its switch entity stays stuck `on`. On a restart — or a Zigbee/Z2M reconnect — the entity reappeared as `unavailable → on`, which the integration mistook for a fresh button press and started a phantom movement: tracked all the way to an endpoint but with **no relay actually fired**, so the reported position silently diverged from the physical cover (for example showing fully open while the cover is closed). That stale-reappearance edge is no longer treated as a command for these relays, since there is no way to reconstruct whether or when a real press happened while Home Assistant was down. Only this configuration is affected — relays that report their OFF come back `off` on restart, so every other mode and the default toggle behaviour are unchanged.

## 4.5.0 (2026-06-22)

### Features

- **Toggle mode: option for relays that pulse but never report their OFF** ([#105](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/105)): the 4.4.0 toggle-mode change drives a still-reported-ON relay OFF before pulsing it ON, to guarantee a clean rising edge. On hardware-managed pulse modules — for example an **Aqara T2** in its 200 ms internal-pulse mode — that pulse the contact themselves but never report the OFF back to Home Assistant, the switch entity stays stuck `on`, so a `turn_off` is not an idempotent "off" but another activation pulse. The integration's clean-edge attempt then lands as a doubled command and the motor's toggle counter drifts — the symptom is Stop reversing the cover and Up driving it down. A new per-cover toggle-mode option, **Relay reports its own OFF** (default **on**, the unchanged 4.4.0 behaviour), can be turned **off** for such modules: toggle mode then only ever sends a single `turn_on` per command and never a `turn_off`, giving exactly one clean activation per press. Configurable from the config card (EN/PT/PL) and as the `relay_reports_off` YAML option.

## 4.4.0 (2026-06-18)

### ⚠ Breaking changes

- **No endpoint stop or run-on for self-stopping motors** ([#105](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/105)): in **Pulse**, **Toggle** and **wrapped-cover** modes the integration no longer sends a relay stop — nor applies endpoint run-on — when a cover reaches the 0%/100% endpoints. These motors stop themselves at their internal limit switches, so the stop was redundant; in **Toggle** mode it was actively wrong, re-pulsing the direction relay and restarting the just-stopped motor (the movement-after-every-full-open/close some users saw). The same now applies to a **separate tilt motor** at its tilt endpoints. **Switch** mode is unchanged — its direction relay is latched ON for the whole movement, so it is still switched off (with run-on) at the endpoint.

  **Migration:** no action is required and no settings are lost. The **Endpoint run-on time** option now applies to **Switch mode only**; it is hidden in the config card for the other modes, and any value previously set on a pulse/toggle/wrapped cover is simply ignored. Mid-travel and mid-tilt stops are unaffected. Sequential closes-then-tilts modes still stop at the closed (0%) endpoint, where the motor is deliberately driven past cover-closed to articulate the slats.

### Features

- **Up/down interlock for switch mode** ([#99](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/99)): in switch (latching relay) mode, when the integration observes one direction relay turn ON from outside Home Assistant — for example a decoupled wall switch, or the physical switch wired straight to the relays — it now turns the opposite direction relay OFF. This covers both the travel (up/down) relays and, on dual-motor covers, the tilt relays, so two opposing relays are never energized at once even on motors with no hardware interlock. The integration already did this when *it* drove the relays; this extends the same protection to externally-triggered changes. Pulse and toggle modes are unaffected, since they don't hold a direction relay on.

### Fixes

- **Switch mode de-energizes after an external open/close** ([#99](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/99)): in switch (latching relay) mode, a movement started from outside Home Assistant — for example toggling the relay's own switch entity, or a wall switch wired straight to it — was tracked to the endpoint but its latched direction relay was never switched off, leaving the motor energized at the limit. Reaching the endpoint now turns that relay off (only the direction still on, never the opposite — and the dual-motor tilt relay likewise), matching what the integration already did for moves it drives itself. Pulse, toggle and wrapped-cover modes are unaffected, since their relays self-release.
- **Dual-motor switch mode: reversing the tilt no longer swallows the next press** ([#99](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/99)): with a separate tilt motor in switch mode, reversing the tilt from outside HA (e.g. tilt-open then tilt-close on the switch entities) left a stale state-change echo queued on the just-released relay, so the *next* external tilt command in the original direction was silently ignored. Externally-triggered tilt-motor moves now only track the motion — the relay is already driven from outside HA — instead of re-firing it, so every press is honoured.
- **Dual-motor switch mode: external cover-open from closed completes in one press** ([#99](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/99)): opening the cover from fully closed via the external switch entity ran the tilt-to-safe pre-step and then stalled, needing a second press to actually travel. Externally-triggered multi-phase moves now continue from the pre-step into the travel phase (and travel pre-step into tilt) the same way UI-initiated moves do.
- **Switch mode: re-driving an already-on relay no longer swallows the next switch event** ([#99](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/99)): the open/close relay senders queued a state-change echo even when the relay was already on — so no event actually fired and the orphaned echo silently swallowed the *next* real switch event. The visible symptom: after an externally-started open continued into travel, switching the relay back off to stop the cover did nothing and it kept opening. The senders now queue an echo only when the relay will actually change state (matching the tilt senders).
- **Separate tilt motor no longer nudges the travel motor** ([#105](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/105)): completing a tilt-only move on a separate-tilt-motor cover previously fell through to the travel stop and re-pulsed the *travel* relay (off a stale last-command), while never issuing a proper tilt stop. Tilt completions now settle the tilt motor directly.
- **Toggle mode no longer uses Pulse time** ([#105](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/105)): toggle-style relays are momentary — a brief press latches the motor and the relay releases itself — so holding the relay ON for the configured **Pulse time** served no purpose. Toggle covers now send a single switch-on per command (briefly switching the relay off first only when it is still reported ON, to guarantee a clean rising edge) and the **Pulse time** option is hidden for toggle mode. **Pulse** mode is unchanged and still uses Pulse time. If you had set a pulse time on a toggle cover it was doing nothing useful, so no action is needed.

## 4.3.0 (2026-06-17)

### Features

- **Configurable assumed state per cover** ([#97](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/97)): a time-based cover dead-reckons its position from travel time with no feedback from the hardware, so Home Assistant treated it as an *assumed state* device — keeping both the open and close controls active at all times. A new device-level **Assumed state** option lets you turn that off when you trust your calibration (for example a wrapped Shelly that reports no position), so Home Assistant greys out actions that can't apply, such as *close* when the cover is already closed. It defaults to **on** for every existing cover, so there's no change unless you opt out, and it's configurable from the config card in all control modes (switch, pulse, toggle, and wrapped). The device-tab toggles also gained click-to-open info tooltips (touch-friendly) behind a `(?)` icon.

### Fixes

- **Rapid toggle presses no longer desync the position** ([#100](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/100)): on toggle-mode covers, pressing open/close/stop faster than the configured pulse time apart could leave the physical motor untouched while the integration began tracking a movement — the reported position then drifted away from where the cover actually was. Each pulse now guarantees a real relay edge (briefly driving the relay off first when a previous pulse is still in flight) so the motor reliably acts on every press.

- **Tilt support for wrapped covers without native tilt** ([#85](https://github.com/Sese-Schneider/ha-cover-time-based/issues/85)): the **Inline** and **Sequential** tilt modes are now available when wrapping an existing cover, even if that cover only exposes open/close/stop. These modes drive the wrapped cover's normal open/close commands, so they work on any cover — previously the tilt options were hidden unless the wrapped cover reported native tilt support. ## 4.0.0 (2026-05-23)

If you're coming from v2.3.2, this is essentially a new integration.

Cover Time Based controls any motor-driven cover from Home Assistant, even when the cover hardware itself can't report its position. It tracks where the cover is by timing how long the motor has been running — and v4 makes that a lot more capable, and a lot easier to live with.

What you get:

- **Configure everything from a dashboard card.** No more YAML. Add a cover under **Settings → Devices & Services → Helpers**, then tune every setting from a built-in card on your dashboard.
- **Calibrate at the click of a button.** Start the motor, let it run, hit stop — the card figures out your travel and tilt times for you.
- **Works with whatever hardware you have.** Latching relays, momentary push-buttons with a separate stop switch, single-button "toggle" controllers, or any existing cover entity you'd like to add position tracking to.
- **Real tilt support.** Venetian blinds, conventional shutters, inverted shutters, and covers with a separate tilt motor — pick the mode that matches your hardware.
- **Stays in sync with physical buttons.** Wall switches and automations driving the motor outside Home Assistant don't throw the position tracker off any more.
- **Survives restarts.** Positions are saved properly and reloaded — even after unexpected shutdowns.
- **Lots of fine-tuning options** for awkward motors: startup delays, minimum movement times, endpoint resync, and more.
- **Translated** into English, Portuguese, and Polish.

### Upgrading from v2.3.2

Your existing YAML configuration still works with a deprecation warning. When you have a moment, recreate your covers via the helper flow so you can manage them through the UI from then on.

This release also includes a long list of smaller fixes, especially around wrapped covers, sequential tilt strategies, and toggle-mode button handling.
